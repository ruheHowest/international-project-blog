Day 1 · Monday

Ready, set, go!

By Rune Herreman
May 20, 2026

The first day of the exchange is finished. With a packed schedule and a long day of traveling behind us, we were all exhausted.

A warm welcome

We started the day off with an introductory session to map out the week. The format is pretty straightforward: daily inspiration sessions or workshops in the morning and afternoon, followed by dedicated group blocks where we actually work on the main assignment.

This year's primary assignment comes from DAF Trucks, and they want us to solve one of two major issues currently hitting the transport industry:

  • Women's safety on the road.
  • Attracting more employees to the truck driving profession.

After the intro we all got up to collect our name tags, and we had some time to mingle with the other exchange students before the first workshop began.

Tesla propaganda

We started off strong, with an inspiration session from Tesla. A few people from the Gigafactory came down to Eindhoven to talk about the software logistics behind their car shipments, diving into things like IoT, factory robotics, and automated tracking. It was definitely interesting to see how they handle scale, but they also pitched a second option for the week's assignment:

Finding the most efficient way to organize outgoing cars in the parking lot for transport pickup.

Honestly, it felt a bit lazy compared to the DAF challenges, so our team was not interested.

Big data

The second session shifted focus towards Big Data — specifically, who is collecting your data, what are they using it for and, most importantly, how can I take control of privacy. While the core topic is always relevant, the presentation felt a bit drawn out for the amount of actual content they covered. Still, it was a decent overview.

European Air Transport Command

Honestly, before this session I did not know the EATC existed, but hearing them talk about how they operate out of Eindhoven Air Base was easily my favourite part of the entire day.

They are a unified seven-nation military alliance that acts as a system to optimize European air mobility. Instead of each country operating expensive fleets, they pool 170 aircraft under a single command structure to do global air transport, mid-flight refuelling, and medical evacuations.

They do this without any money changing hands. The nations request flights and exchange services using an in-house software system called MEAT and a virtual currency called ATARES. You earn credits when you fly a mission for a partner nation and spend them when you need assets from another country.

Again, this was the coolest inspiration session by far.

Cultural Tasting

After a day of listening, planning, and meeting our teams for the week, it was time for a good hangout. Every school's group was supposed to bring a delicacy from their home country or city for all to taste. This ended up being a bit funny since there were 4 schools from Belgium, meaning a lot of chocolate was eaten.

This was also the perfect place to meet new people. Every person there was open for a chat and I can confidently say we made some real connections.

Day 2 · Tuesday

Finding Our Rhythm

By Dmytro Kyrylets
May 21, 2026

Day two turned out to be a pretty solid one.

Group work

We started at 8:45 and got straight into group work. By that point we knew each other a little bit, and it showed. The second session just felt easier and more comfortable than the first.

Escape room

Then came the escape room. It was fully cyber-security themed. The goal? Find all the hidden clues, stop the hacker from detonating a massive bomb, and get educated on about online-safety. It wasn't about skills or knowledge but about working as a team. I would say it was one of the most fun things we did during this international project.

Strijp-S and Strijp-T

After lunch, we went on a tour of Strijp-S and Strijp-T, which belonged to a large Phillips factory complex a long time ago. People called it the "Forbidden City." Hard to imagine that now, walking through what has basically become a cool urban neighbourhood.

The city center

After dinner at the food truck outside, which was very tasty, we went on a tour of the city with Paul Deckers. He showed us all the historical sights: the places that were flattened in WWII, the ruins from medieval Eindhoven, the new cultural center.

We ended up at St. Catharine's Church. What got us was the floor near the entrance, there is an area on the steps, below your feet, where you can still see remains of people buried centuries ago. None of us saw that coming.

Day 5 · Friday

Wrapping It All Up

By Muhammad Shayan
May 24, 2026

The final day was a mix of excitement and just a bit of nervous energy.

Last minute improvements

We started by heading back to the event center where we had been working throughout the project. First we had to review our project and make some last minute improvements. My team and I added a few new ideas to our Tesla project, especially focusing on solving the parking problem more effectively.

Presentations

Once everything was ready, we realized one of our group members was running late, so we waited for him before moving ahead. As soon as he arrived, we went straight into our presentation. All the groups presented their projects, and it was really impressive to see how innovative and creative everyone's solutions were.

The results

Then came the moment we were all waiting for — the results. We were happy to receive our certificates, but the biggest surprise was that we secured first place for the tesla challenge! As a prize, we even received Tesla socks, which made the win even more memorable.

Goodbye, Eindhoven

After that, lunch was served, and it was perfect. It gave us a chance to relax and talk about the whole experience.

Finally, it was time to say goodbye. We wrapped up the day with lots of good memories, feeling proud of what we had achieved and the great time we spent together.
