Day 1 · Monday

Ready, set, go!

By Rune Herreman
March 9, 2026

The first day of the exchange is finished. With a packed schedule and a long day of traveling behind us, we were all exhausted.

A warm welcome

We started the day off with an introductory session to map out the week. The format is pretty straightforward: daily inspiration sessions or workshops in the morning and afternoon, followed by dedicated group blocks where we actually work on the main assignment.

This year's primary assignment comes from DAF Trucks, and they want us to solve one of two major issues currently hitting the transport industry:

  • Women's safety on the road.
  • Attracting more employees to the truck driving profession.

After the intro, we all got up to collect our name tags, and we had some time to mingle with the other exchange students before the first workshop began.

Tesla propaganda

We started off strong, with an inspiration session from Tesla. A few people from the Gigafactory came down to Eindhoven to talk about the software logistics behind their car shipments, diving into things like IoT, factory robotics, and automated tracking. It was definitely interesting to see how they handle scale, but they also pitched a second option for the week's assignment:

Finding the most efficient way to organize outgoing cars in the parking lot for transport pickup.

Honestly, it felt a bit lazy compared to the DAF challenges, so our team was not interested.

Big data

The second session shifted focus towards Big Data, specifically, who is collecting your data, what they are using it for, and, most importantly, how you can take control of your privacy. While the core topic is always relevant, the presentation felt a bit drawn out for the amount of actual content they covered. Still, it was a decent overview.

European Air Transport Command

Honestly, before this session, I did not know the EATC existed, but hearing them talk about how they operate out of Eindhoven Air Base was easily my favorite part of the entire day.

They are a unified seven-nation military alliance that acts as a system to optimize European air mobility. Instead of each country operating expensive fleets, they pool 170 aircraft under a single command structure to do global air transport, mid-flight refueling, and medical evacuations.

They do this without any money changing hands. The nations request flights and exchange services using an in-house software system called MEAT and a virtual currency called ATARES. You earn credits when you fly a mission for a partner nation and spend them when you need assets from another country.

Again, this was the coolest inspiration session by far.

Cultural Tasting

After a day of listening, planning, and meeting our teams for the week, it was time for a good hangout. Every school's group was supposed to bring a delicacy from their home country or city for all to taste. This ended up being a bit funny since there were 4 schools from Belgium, meaning a lot of chocolate was eaten.

This was also the perfect place to meet new people. Every person there was open for a chat and I can confidently say we made some real connections.

Day 2 · Tuesday

Finding Our Rhythm

By Dmytro Kyrylets
March 10, 2026

Day two turned out to be a pretty solid one.

Group work

We started at 8:45 and got straight into group work. By that point, we knew each other a little bit, and it showed. The second session just felt easier and more comfortable than the first.

Escape room

Then came the escape room. It was fully cyber-security themed. The goal? Find all the hidden clues, stop the hacker from detonating a massive bomb, and get educated about online safety. It wasn't about skills or knowledge but about working as a team. I would say it was one of the most fun things we did during this international project.

Strijp-S and Strijp-T

After lunch, we went on a tour of Strijp-S and Strijp-T, which belonged to a large Philips factory complex a long time ago. People called it the "Forbidden City." Hard to imagine that now, walking through what has basically become a cool urban neighborhood.

A tour of the city

After dinner at the food truck outside, which was very tasty, we went on a tour of the city with Paul Deckers. He showed us all the historical sights: the places that were flattened in WWII, the ruins from medieval Eindhoven, the new cultural center.

We ended up at St. Catherine's Church. What got us was the floor near the entrance: there is an area on the steps, below your feet, where you can still see remains of people buried centuries ago. None of us saw that coming.

Day 3 · Wednesday

Sustainable Development Goals and IoT

By Simon Cornelissis
March 11, 2026

After walking through the rain (thank you, The Netherlands), we were ready for an Internet of Things workshop.

IoT at the intersection

We were introduced to the ESP32 Microcontroller, message brokers, and traffic controllers. Our task? Set up a simulated intersection where traffic lights would communicate their light color to the cars at the intersection. Working with microcontrollers also sparked some ideas for additional features we could incorporate into the overarching assignment of the week.

Lunchtime

Pasta, tomato sauce: the typical Dutch lunch. We actually ran a little low on food since we kept meeting new people and just couldn't stop talking. Thank you, Erasmus.

Sustainable Development Goals

The next inspiration session covered the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals. It was eye-opening to see how the UN has laid out concrete goals for countries to build a more sustainable world, addressing everything from climate change to inequality, both on an individual and organizational level.

We also got into digital sovereignty and alternative AI models coming out of Europe. One topic that came up was the deal made between OpenAI and the US Department of Defense involving autonomous weapons and mass surveillance. It was a sobering reminder that choosing which AI model you use actually carries weight.

Choosing which AI model you use does matter.

After that heavier material, our groups sat together and talked through the climate disasters we've witnessed in recent years. One recurring frustration was that governments remain so hesitant about nuclear energy, despite it being one of the safest, most profitable, and most eco-friendly energy sources available. The lingering shadow of Chernobyl and Fukushima keeps politicians from making the obvious call. What a shame.

A well-earned break

The day wrapped up a little earlier than usual, and honestly, it was welcome. We were running on fumes but felt satisfied with everything we'd taken in. Back at our stay, we gave ourselves a break from worrying about climate change, put on Community, and made hagelslag sandwiches. Chocolate sprinkles on bread: thoroughly enjoyed the local Dutch cuisine.

Day 4 · Thursday

The Final Stretch

By Yoni Furniere
March 12, 2026

Today was the final stretch for our project. We did some fine-tuning and started preparing our presentation for the next day. In between, we also had two workshops.

Cloud workshop

During the first workshop, they taught us everything the cloud has to offer.

What makes the cloud so interesting is that the cloud provider will provide you with underlying infrastructure without you needing to worry about any of its maintenance. Another advantage is that you only pay for what you use. The focus was on Azure, a product from Microsoft that makes it possible to create and manage everything cloud-related. Of course, it wouldn't have been a workshop without some hands-on labs. We deployed a simple website by following the instructions they gave us.

Neither the lecture nor the lab was really challenging because we'd already seen this in System Architecture and Deployment.

Yet another AI session

We also had a workshop about AI. Nothing special. Just another session praising how great AI is.

The 'Better Late Than Never' Eindhoven Exploration Tour

After we finished our presentation preparations, we were free to join a teacher at Fontys for an exploration walk in Eindhoven.

Fun fact about the Eindhoven stadium: they built a bridge between the station and the stadium to keep the supporters out of the city. The locals of Eindhoven often say that the station is designed to look like a certain old Philips radio, but this is actually a myth, as the radio was released after the station was built. The city of Eindhoven was hit pretty hard during WW2, so they had to rebuild. They didn't really enforce an architectural style, so architects just did whatever they felt like doing, which resulted in architectural chaos.

Italian dinner

We ended the walk at the St. Catherine's Church, where we got some free time to explore the area. Finally, we ate at an Italian restaurant called Happy Italy, where we could order a free pizza. I wasn't really a fan of the pizza options, so I took a Spaghetti Carbonara. I have to say, it wasn't bad, but I prefer the Carbonara pasta from Howest.

End of the day

After a full day of hard work and workshops, we ended our day by watching a few episodes of Community.

Day 5 · Friday

Wrapping It All Up

By Muhammad Shayan
March 13, 2026

The final day was a mix of excitement and just a bit of nervous energy.

Getting ready to present

We started by heading back to the event center where we had been working throughout the project. First, we had to review our project and make some last-minute improvements. My team and I added a few new ideas to our Tesla project, focusing on solving the parking problem more effectively.

Once everything was ready, we realized one of our group members was running late, so we waited for him before moving ahead. As soon as he arrived, we went straight into our presentation. All the groups presented their projects, and it was really impressive to see how innovative and creative everyone's solutions were.

The results. Drum roll, please!

Then came the moment we were all waiting for. Who were the winners of the challenge? We were happy to receive our certificates, but the biggest surprise was that we secured first place for the Tesla challenge! As a prize, we even received Tesla socks, which made the win even more memorable.

Goodbye, Eindhoven

After that, lunch was served, and it was perfect. It gave us a chance to relax and talk about the whole experience.

Finally, it was time to say goodbye. We wrapped up the day with lots of good memories, feeling proud of what we had achieved and the great time we spent together.
